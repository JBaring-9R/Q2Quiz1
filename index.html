Philippine Historical Figures

~Image~ ~Name~ ~Timeline~ ~Accomplishments~
Jose Rizal June 19, 1861 - December 30, 1896 Jose Rizal's accomplishments were his literary works such as his 2 most famous novels: Noli Me Tangere and El Filibusterismo; the founding of La Liga Filipina which aimed at promoting social reforms through peaceful means; and his political ideas which inspired other Filipinos such as Andres Bonifacio and Emilio Aguinaldo.
Andres Bonifacio November 30, 1863 - May 10, 1897 Andres Bonifacio was the founder of the Katipunan, a secret revolutionary society that led the rebellion against Spain, whose goal was to free the Philippines from Spanish rule and declare independence by means of a revolution.
Emilio Aguinaldo March 22, 1869 - February 6, 1964 Emilio Aguinaldo was the first president of the Philippines. Thanks to his leadership skills, he led the Filipinos into numerous battles and declared Philippine independence on June 12, 1898.
Apolinario Mabini July 23, 1864 - May 13, 1903 Apolinario Mabini was the chief adviser of Emilio Aguinaldo during the Philippine Revolution against Spain. He drafted decrees and crafted the constitution for the First Philippine Republic, including the framework of the revolutionary government which was implemented in Malolos, Bulacan in 1899.
Marcelo H. del Pilar August 30, 1850 - July 4, 1896 Marcelo H. del Pilar founded the Diariong Tagalog where he exposed the the atrocities committed by the Spanish friars against Filipinos. Because of this, the friars were scared of a revolution happening and they pressured the government to take action. Del Pilar escaped to Spain where he joined the Filipino Propaganda Movement and became the editor-in-chief of La Solidaridad. He also inspired the Katipunan to revolt against Spanish rule.
